Working Papers

Inclusion to Exclude: How Femonationalism Impacts Policy Preferences

Sophie Mainz
Presented at American Political Science Association Annual Meeting, 2024

This paper provides causal evidence from a preregistered survey experiment with 3,118 U.S. citizens, showing that femonationalist rhetoric can mobilize support for illiberal policies in defense of progressive gender achievements.

Trading Principles? How Linking Immigration to Liberal Values Shifts Public Preferences

Sophie Mainz
Presented at European Political Science Association Annual Conference, 2024

This study provides causal evidence from an original preregistered candidate-choice experiment with 1,500 UK citizens, showing that issue trade-offs influence preferences on immigration policies.

"Not in Her Name": Strategic Rebuttals to the Gendered Defence of Illiberal Exclusion

Sophie Mainz, Michal Grahn, Katharina Lawall, and Stuart J. Turnbull-Dugarte
Presented at American Political Science Association Annual Meeting, 2025

Drawing on a pre-registered audio-visual vignette experiment with 3,994 German adults, we test whether different forms of counter-speech can reduce agreement with femonationalist claims.

Public Support for Social Media Regulation

Sophie Mainz

Employing conjoint experiments in the UK and Germany, this study examines under which conditions citizens support increased regulation of social media platforms.
